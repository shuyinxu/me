The Problem

When we took on this project our client gave us a variety of jobs to choose from, which we narrowed down to a website redesign and creation of a budeting portal.

We decided on a budgeting portal because many of Rye Financial’s clients operate on a budget based system and lack a way to manage their expenses and revenue in relation to their budget.

Through this portal, we hope that businesses will be able to better visualize their budgeting problems and understand their financial status with more ease.

Our Client

Our client, Rye Financial, is founded by Stephen Rye Switzer, an independent accountant. He provides accounting and bookkeeping services for small business, non-profit organizations, and B-corps in the Bay Area.

Our goal for this project is to design a user portal that improves Stephen’s service and report delievery while allowing his users to more efficiently monitor their budgeting and finances through a personalized system.

User Research & Synthesis

To better understand the current bookkeeping experiences for accountants and financial advisors working at our target organizations, we created an interview guide that includes nine questions as shown below. The questions specifically address:
— what type of critical statistics they expect to see from financial reports
— how they interact with the bookkeeping tools
— how they categorize their transactions
— how they maintain and oversee their budgets.
To draw synthesis from our interviews, we first created an affinity map to group our insights into different categories and find patterns.
Based on the affinity map, we were able to draw the following insights:
— The main tools that users are using are spreadsheet and Quickbooks
— Most users are happy with their current bookkeeping system, so there is no need for new bookkeeping portal that repeat the same functions
— But many users are interested in having a tool that can help them keep track of budget
— They also expressed interest in viewing data across various time periods

Ideation

We identified a potential work flow and began ideating for a user portal that includes a budget maintenance system with data visualization features in addition to the spreadsheets that Stephen currently uses to communicate with his clients.
Then we brainstormed on ways to visualize various types of data and created low-fidelity wireframes to test out different page layouts.

Mid-fidelity Wireframes

After looking at the various ideations, we picked out a few key functionalities from each design and made our very first model of the user portal by combining ideas and features from all of them. Some key features are:
— Notification system
— Budget alert bar
— Expenses and revenue overview

User Testing

Through UserTesting.com and in-person user testing, we tested our mid-fidelity protytpe with users in the 20s and 30s age range from the United States. The task we assigned to them was asking them to monitor and set a budget goal for any expense category.

Usertesting.com recording

From these results, we were able to pinpoint main troubles such as navigating through the portal, performing tasks such as viewing expenses for a certain category, and editing budgets. We modified our “Edit Budget” button and changed the layout of the revenue and expenses overviews so that users could find them more easily.

Final Product

Our client also asked for a redesign for his current website—he wanted to increase its credibility and upgrade it to be more business-professional.

In our redesign, we added more visuals and illustrations that would help reduce the denseness from having too much text and redesigned the “Service Plan”, “About”, and “Content” page. We also added a custom service plans page that allows users that do not necessarily belong to the preset business types written on the “Service Plan” page to fill out a survey form.
